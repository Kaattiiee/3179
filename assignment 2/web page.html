Australia’s Tourism Flows: Inbound and Outbound Journeys

Australia, a country renowned for its rich and diverse tourism offerings, attracting millions of travellers each year. Between 2017 and 2025, Australia's tourism experienced both growth and challenges. In 2021, the COVID-19 pandemic led to a sharp decline in international travel, affecting both inbound and outbound travelling. However, the industry is recovering in the years followed and returning to levels close to those before the pandemic.

Inbound Tourist to Australia

Between March 2024 and March 2025, a total of almost 5 million tourists visited Australia. Among the recorded countries of residence, over 8 hundred thousand tourists were from New Zealand, followed by China with more than 480,000 tourists. The total spending of these tourists while in Australia was also recorded. Tourists from China spent the most with over 7 billion dollars, followed by tourists from the USA, who spent over 3 billion dollars. While China contributed both a large number of tourists and the highest total spending, New Zealand tourists spent less on average despite their large numbers.

Tourist spending generally increases with the length of stay, but patterns vary by market. Tourists from China stand out with the highest spending despite moderate stays, reflecting their strong spending on shopping and experiences. In contrast, visitors from New Zealand tend to have more visits but spend less on average. In addition, tourists from the USA and UK combine long visits and high spendings, making them key contributors to Australia's tourism industry.

Travel peaks also varies from country to country. Countries near Australia like New Zealand has relatively consistent tourist arrivals throughout the year. For Asian countries, particularly China and Japan, seasonal peak occurs during holiday periods such as Lunar New Year and summer vacations. European countries tend to have more visitors during the Norther Hemisphere's winter months, aligning with Australia's summer season.

Outbound Tourist from Australia

In terms of travel destinations for Australians, Indonesia ranks at the top, with over 1.4 million visitors between March 2024 to March 2025. New Zealand, which is also the leading source of inbound tourists to Australia, remains a high popularity in travel destination choices for Australians. There's also over 1.1 million tourists that were attracted to New Zealand over the same period. Other favourite destinations including Japan, the USA, Thailand and China reflecting a mixture of preferences for travel destinations for Australians.

Among Australians who travelled internationally, the regional distribution of departures highlights where the majority of tourists travel from in Australia. Unsurprisingly, New South Wales and Victoria as Australia's most popular states, recorded the highest numbers of outbound tourists. This reflects not only their large population, but also their international connectivity with major flight routes. Other states and territories contribute smaller shares, highlighted the significant role NSW and Vic play in facilitating Australia's outbound travel.

Travel destinations for Australians also vary by season. Similar to inbound tourism, New Zealand remains a consistently popular destination throughout the year. Canada, with its opposite seasons to Australia, attracts many tourists seeking different weather condition abroad. Other destinations across Europe and Asia, such as the UK, Japan, Thailand and China, also receive a significant number of Australian tourists, reflecting a diverse travel interests for Australians.

Economic Significance of Tourism

Overall, Australia's outbound spending consistently exceeds inbound spending, indicating Australian tourists spend more abroad than the country earns from inbound tourists each year. The steady increase of spendings from 2016 had a slight decrease in 2019, followed by a sharp drop in 2020. Both inbound and outbound spend fell significantly, reflecting the impact of internation travel restrictions during the COVID-19 pandemic. After the pandemic, the spendings were continuously increasing, signaling economic recovery across all countries.

Education services, a major source of income for Australia, rely heavily on international students coming into the country to study. Alongside education, rental income from vacation homes has also became a significant contributor. For short-term tourists, categories such as accommodation services, food and beverages, shopping, gifts and souvenirs are more representiative of their spending areas. However, education services and vacation home rentals dominated international visitor expenditures including both long-term visitors and tourists.

Although inbound tourists spent less than outbound tourists, they contributed signficantly to employment in tourism-related industries. In 2020, with reduced tourist activity, the number of jobs in these industries also declined. Overall, tourism supported a large amount of jobs, with Accommodation, Retail trade and Cafes, restaurants and takeaway food services sectors employing the most. A large number of filled positions can potentially indicate stronger economic benefits for Australia.

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics by Australia’s national statistical agency & Tourism Research Australia by Australian Government
